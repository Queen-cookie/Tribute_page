  • 1895:

    The most prominent Sheikh of the Salihiyya order was the Sayyid who arrived in Berbera in 1895 and constructed his own mosque and began propagating.


  • 1899:

    In one of his letters to Sultan Deria in 1899, Sayyid Hassan said that the British "have destroyed our religion and made our children their children" alluding to Sultan Nur's incident with the Roman French Mission at Berbera.


  • 1917:

    In 1917, the Ottoman Empire referred to Hassan as the "Emir of the Somali".


  • 1919:

    By 1919, despite the British having built large stone forts to guard the passes to the hills, Hassan and his armed bands were at large, robbing and killing.


  • 1920:

    On 21 December 1920, Hassan died of influenza at the age of 64, his grave is believed to be somewhere close to Imi town of the Somali Region of Ethiopia; however, the exact spot of the Sayid's grave is unknown.
